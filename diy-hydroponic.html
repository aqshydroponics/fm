DIY Hydroponics

Step 1: Arrange Net Cups

Space the net cups according to the mature size of the plants you intend to grow. Mark the position on the lid using a pencil.

Step 2: Drill Holes in Lid

Slide a scrap piece of wood beneath the lid and use a drill fitted with a hole saw bit to carefully drill out the holes for the net cups.

Pro Safety Tip: When drilling plastic with a hole saw bit, the slower the bit speed the better.

Step 3: Clean Up the Holes

Use a utility knife to carefully cut away any stray plastic pieces, then sand away the remainder.

Step 4: Install Air Pump

Drill a hole in the top of the lid with a bit that matches the size of your air pump’s hose. Slide the hose through and attach the airstone.

Step 5: Fill Container With Nutrient-Rich Water

Fill the container with water and add the nutrients following the manufacturer’s instructions. Turn the pump on to begin oxygenating the water.

Step 6: Add Plants

Place your plants in the net cups and slide them into the holes cut in the lid.

Tips for Successful Hydroponic Gardening

Maintain the Proper pH: The water's pH should be kept between 5.5 and 6.5 for plants to absorb nutrients properly.

Use Empty Water: Hydroponic nutrients work best in water with very few minerals (close to 0 ppm). Distilled, reverse osmosis, or captured rainwater are ideal.

Regularly Change the Nutrient Solution: Replace the nutrient solution weekly or bi-weekly. Always drain and clean the reservoir instead of just topping it up.

Give Your Plants Plenty of Light: Provide natural light or grow lights for best growth results.

Tailor Nutrients to Plants: Use more nitrogen in vegetative stages, more phosphorus and potassium in flowering/fruiting. Large fruiting plants may also need extra calcium and magnesium.
